CHANGING USER BEHAVIOR IS A LONG PROCESS

Reduce the likelihood of becoming a victim of cyber-attacks by training your employees and building a long-term awareness program.

Once we understand your compliance requirements, available resources, and general goals, we can define the roadmap for the awareness plan. Next step is setting up a long-term awareness campaign in the required languages for each department.

Based on key indicators, we measure the training success and identify risky users. The goal is clear: reduce the number of unsafe employees and have less security related incidents in your organization.

OUR AWARENESS TRAINING

CYBER SECURITY AWARENESS TRAINING THAT GOES BEYOND THE TOPIC OF PHISHING

LUCY comes with more than 250 out-of-the-box awareness trainings in the form of posters, screensavers, flyers, games, PowerPoint presentations, and many more.

We cover all relevant security topics in up to 130 languages.

  • Download Training Catalogue (pdf)
  • TRAINING PREVIEW

RUN THE TRAINING IN A SAAS ENVIROMENT OR ON-PREMISES

You can run our content in the cloud as a SaaS or export all trainings (SCORM) and run them on-site. LUCY allows you to track training success, synchronize your employees automatically via LDAP, send automatic reminders to users who didn't start the training, and create automatic campaign reports.

CUSTOMIZATION MAKES ALL THE DIFFERENCE

Each course can be easily adapted by our team or yours using a build-in awareness authoring toolkit. In LUCY you have the ability to add customized content in the existing courses. For example, you could ask your employees to read and acknowledge your internal policies prior to starting the course or add a personal greeting note from your CEO at the beginning of the training.

How does it work?

Step

№1

SET GOALS & CREATE A PLAN

Understanding Your Goals Will Define Program Scope

A well-documented plan will assure that goals become measurable and your investment in security awareness proves its value.

  • Understand your compliance requirements, available resources, and budget restrictions. Define the roadmap and build the awareness plan.
  • Define target groups, required languages, and other training constraints. Work out key indicators to measure the training success. Define training topics.

step

№2

ASSESS AWARENESS LEVEL

Use phishing simulations or assessments to understand the current awareness level

In order to improve user behavior, we recommend measuring the current awareness level. This can be done through attack simulations or exams.

  • Perform sample phishing simulations with employees from different departments.
  • Create interactive exams where users can test their knowledge.

step

№3

RUN THE AWARENESS CAMPAIGN

Improve the security awareness level with tailored courses. Optimize the program over time.

The awareness campaign is an ongoing process and involves more than just running a few eLearning trainings. It is a long-term commitment to change company culture.

  • Create role-based awareness trainings for different departments and measure progress. Use different tools like posters, videos, and interactive elements to keep the user engaged.
  • Analyze the results and adjust the training program. Assess the awareness level again with exams or attack simulations and measure program effectiveness.
